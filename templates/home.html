Delete Employee

The employer has to prove that the firing was based on specific behavior and not antipathy toward a group or class of people. Legally, this is described as firing “for cause.”
Why Delete Employee?

1. Incompetence, including lack of productivity.
2. Insubordination and related issues such as dishonesty.
3. Attendance issues, such as frequent absences or chronic tardiness.
4. Theft or other criminal behavior including revealing trade secrets.
5. Physical violence or threats against other employees.
